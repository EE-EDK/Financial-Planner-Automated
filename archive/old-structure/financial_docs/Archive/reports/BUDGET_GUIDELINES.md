## THE "EVERYTHING ELSE" PROBLEM

### What "Everything Else" Typically Includes:
- Random Amazon purchases
- Impulse buys
- Gifts
- Home improvement
- Miscellaneous shopping
- "Just this once" purchases

### Current Reality:
**You spend $1,300+/month in "Everything Else"**

### Target:
**Reduce to $100-200/month maximum**

### How to Control "Everything Else":

1. **Delete Apps**
   - Delete Amazon app
   - Delete shopping apps (Target, Walmart, etc.)
   - Delete food delivery apps

2. **Remove Saved Payment Info**
   - Remove from browsers
   - Remove from websites
   - Make it harder to impulse buy

3. **30-Day Rule**
   - Want something? Wait 30 days
   - Still want it after 30 days? Then consider
   - Most "wants" disappear in 30 days

4. **Before ANY Purchase**:
   - Is this in my budget?
   - Do I NEED this or WANT this?
   - Can I wait 30 days?
   - Is there a free alternative?
   - What am I trading for this? (debt payoff, emergency fund)

## SPENDING TRIGGERS & ALTERNATIVES

### Common Spending Triggers:

**Trigger: Boredom/Idle Time**
- Old Behavior: Browse Amazon, buy stuff
- New Behavior: Go for walk, read library book, exercise

**Trigger: Work Stress**
- Old Behavior: Order takeout, online shopping
- New Behavior: Quick home-cooked meal, meal prep in advance

**Trigger: Reward Mentality**
- Old Behavior: "I deserve this" purchase
- New Behavior: Free reward (movie at home, park visit)

**Trigger: Social Pressure**
- Old Behavior: Eat out with friends, expensive activities
- New Behavior: Suggest free alternatives (walk in park, potluck)

**Trigger: Fatigue/Exhaustion**
- Old Behavior: Fast food, delivery apps
- New Behavior: Keep frozen meals for emergencies, breakfast for dinner

### Alternative Behaviors:

**Instead of Shopping:**
- Declutter and organize what you have
- "Shop your closet" - rediscover forgotten items
- Sell unused items (make money instead of spending)
- DIY with materials you already own

**Instead of Dining Out:**
- 15-minute quick meals (keep ingredients on hand)
- Slow cooker meals (prep in morning)
- Breakfast for dinner (easy and cheap)
- Frozen prepared meals (cheaper than takeout)

**Instead of Entertainment Spending:**
- Free museums and parks
- Library (books, movies, audiobooks)
- Free community events
- At-home movie nights
